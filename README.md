# Loom — a co-writing studio

Loom is a studio for writing a novel with an AI author, two ways. **Write together** and you trade passages — you write, and the AI answers in your voice, at about the same length, matching the genre and tone you've established. Or **guide the story** and you direct while the AI writes every line, section by section, from your instructions. Either way the book is woven into one voice and read as a real, page-turning manuscript.

## Two ways to write

When you start a book, Loom asks how you want to make it:

- **Write together (participate)** — you and the AI author trade passages in one shared voice. You write; it continues in your style and at your length.
- **Guide the story (guide)** — you direct, the AI author writes. You describe what should happen next and it writes the next ~275-word section. Instead of a paper/material choice, the setup offers a **writing style** (Literary, Cinematic, Cozy, Noir, Whimsical, Adventure, Gothic, Minimalist), plus **point of view**, **tense**, and — for adult books — an **adult-audiences** toggle with **violence/gore, explicitness, and language** intensity sliders (off by default; all characters written as adults). The reader's notes (genre, prose voice, synopsis, story memory, craft) update as the story develops, the page-based reading/navigation is identical, and each section shows the direction that produced it. After the first section, the next direction box is **pre-filled with a suggested direction** the AI proposes — accept it to continue the arc, or rewrite it to steer your own way. **Section length** is adjustable from 150–400 words (default 275). Both the writing style and these guide options stay editable later from Settings. In guide mode the **Craft** note is relabeled **Story & direction** and scores the story and your directorial choices — not the prose, since the AI writes it and shouldn't grade its own writing.

## What's inside

- **Title page setup** — name the book and author, pick a cover, page format, material, typeface (serif, sans, mono, storybook, or **cursive**), **ink color**, and reading size. A **live preview** updates as you choose, both here and in **Settings**, so you see the page before you commit. Sensible defaults are pre-selected, so you can just begin. Everything is editable later, on any page.
- **Writing & directing** — in *participate* mode, write your passage and hand it to the AI author; it continues for roughly the same number of words, in the same style. In *guide* mode, write a direction and the AI writes the next section. The prose **streams in live** as it's written, then the view settles on where the new text begins with a brief reveal animation.
- **Regenerate** — don't love the latest passage? Re-roll it with one tap; the AI writes a fresh take from the same point (the prior version is kept in history).
- **Rework this passage** — a per-passage workbench on every page. **AI rewrite**: give an instruction ("make this scene tenser"), and tailor it with **length** (shorter / same / longer) and **scope** (light touch keeps every event and the structure; free hand may restructure the passage) — the controls alone are enough, no instruction required. Or switch to **Edit by hand** and tweak the text directly, saved exactly as typed. Either way only that passage changes, it must still fit seamlessly between its neighbors, and the prior version is snapshotted to History.
- **A living editor's loop** — the reader's-notes critique and a running list of recently used imagery *and sentence constructions* are fed back into every generation, so each section actively works on the story's current weakness and stops reaching for the same pet images, phrases, and rhythms. The AI also always knows *where* it is — which chapter, how deep into it, how long the book is — so scene-versus-summary balance and chapter cadence stay honest.
- **An anti-AI-prose toolkit** — a deterministic **tell-linter** scans every new section for machine-writing patterns (uniform sentence rhythm, "not X, but Y" contrasts, rule-of-three lists, dramatic one-line paragraphs, anaphora runs, stock body language) and shows the findings in a **Prose tells** card in the notes, with a one-tap **Fix these** button that line-edits the section in place to repair exactly those findings (story events and length preserved; the prior version goes to History). With the polish pass on, new sections get the same treatment automatically. **Section shape memory** notices when consecutive sections open or close the same way and steers the next one differently, and a rotating **texture note** (dialogue-heavy, no similes, plain summary, one long unbroken paragraph…) breaks the model's house style section by section. Dialogue gets its own guidance — interruptions, deflections, distinct per-character voices tracked in the notes — and each guide style ships with a built-in **voice exemplar** so every book gets voice anchoring even without a pasted sample.
- **Second take** — an optional Settings toggle: the AI drafts each section twice from genuinely different angles, and an editor model keeps whichever take reads more human. Doubles drafting cost; off by default. Stacks with the polish pass (two takes → pick → line-edit).
- **Author's canon** — pin the facts the AI must never get wrong (names, dates, who knows what) in an editable card in the notes panel. Your canon is authoritative: it outranks the AI's own story memory on any conflict.
- **Voice sample** — optionally paste a short passage (yours, or a public-domain excerpt) in Settings; the AI matches its rhythm and diction, which anchors voice far better than a style label. Style only — its content is never reused.
- **Polish pass** — an optional Settings toggle: after drafting each section, the AI gives it one careful line-editing pass (varied rhythm, no filler, no recycled imagery) before it lands. Roughly doubles a section's time and cost, so it's off by default.
- **Real-book pages** — the manuscript flows into fixed-size pages like a printed book (text reflows and splits across pages exactly to fit). In participate mode a translucent inset box and a "spine ledger" show which hand wrote which thread; in guide mode the prose reads as one continuous voice. Turning pages plays a 3D page-flip animation, and on touch you can swipe between pages.
- **Reader's notes** — the AI author keeps a live read on the prose voice, the genre, a running synopsis, story memory (cast, places, facts, open threads), and a craft/story score — paired with a short critique of what's holding the story back — updated after every exchange.
- **Navigate & fork** — page back and forth through the book, with fast skip-ahead and jump-to-end controls on longer books. "Edit from here" pulls a passage (or a direction) back into the editor and discards everything after it — the book forks at that point.
- **Chapters** — mark a new chapter while you write, or manage them later from the **Chapters** panel: add, rename, remove, and jump to any chapter. Chapters open on a fresh page, with a heading, on screen, in the reader, and in the PDF. Decide a chapter break belongs *mid-passage*, long after the fact? **"❡ Chapter here"** on any page opens a paragraph picker — the passage is split at the paragraph you choose and the new chapter starts there (reversible from History).
- **Find & bookmarks** — search the whole book from the **Find** panel; results show highlighted snippets and jump to the exact page. A ribbon in the top corner of every page bookmarks the passage (saved on your device), and your bookmarks live in the same panel.
- **Full-text editing** — revise the whole manuscript at once; `## Chapter` marker lines keep chapter breaks in place so you can move, add, or remove them right in the text.
- **Revision history** — Loom snapshots the book before risky changes (full-text edits, regenerations, chapter changes, trims) so you can roll back to any recent version; restoring is itself reversible.
- **Cast sheet** — first-class character entries (name, role, voice, notes) in a **Cast** drawer. The AI proposes entries from the manuscript; nothing lands without your confirmation, every field is editable, and the sheet is sent — as authoritative — with every section, rewrite, and consistency check.
- **Consistency check** — one tap in the notes scans the whole book for contradictions (names, timelines, facts, who-knows-what) against itself, your canon, and the cast sheet, and lists the findings with jump-to-passage links.
- **Score over time & pacing** — the reader's-notes score is recorded after every section and drawn as a small chart in the notes, alongside words-per-chapter bars, so you can watch the story strengthen (or sag) as it grows.
- **Read-only share link** — publish a book at a clean public reader URL (`/read/…`) for others to read in its book form, with no editing or controls; toggle it off any time to revoke access.
- **Read aloud** — narrate the book with one tap: it starts from the current page and auto-advances, turning pages as it goes, until the end of the story or you stop. Uses ElevenLabs text-to-speech (set `ELEVENLABS_API_KEY`; optional `ELEVENLABS_VOICE_ID` / `ELEVENLABS_MODEL_ID`).
- **PDF & EPUB export** — export through a print view that honors your cover, format, material, typeface, and size, or download a chaptered EPUB for e-readers.
- **Audiobook export** — download each chapter as a narrated MP3 in the same ElevenLabs voice as read-aloud (long chapters are synthesized in parts and stitched). Requires `ELEVENLABS_API_KEY`.
- **Library** — an `admin · all books` view lists every book with its word count and last-edited time, where you can open or delete any book. It can be protected with an optional admin password.
